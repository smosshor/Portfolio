About me

Senior at Carnegie Mellon University Studying Decision Science and Human-Computer Interaction

I am passionate about the intersections between education and technology, within a complex landscape of secondary education systems, social government programs and sustainable enterprises. My diverse work experience, including conducting Human Factors usability tests in the medical field, teaching writing to urban middle school students and running quantitative data analysis on casual theoretical questions enables me to succeed in multi-faceted roles.

In my free time, I love backbacking, painting, traveling, yoga, running and good food.

Things I like to Do

Research

I enjoy using quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis tools; from conducting interviews and focus groups, coding data, designing lab studies and mining datasets.

Human Centered Design

Understanding user experiences through diverse tools such as contextual inquiry, usability testing, storyboarding and interviews helps me gain insight for better design.

Educational Design

In particular, I love designing aligned curriculums and creating instructional tools that break down big ideas in clear lessons, designed with students in mind.
