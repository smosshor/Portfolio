Animal Cop

Animal Cop is a digital reporting system for concerned citizens and a management application for Humane Officers, created for the Pittsburgh Humane Animal Rescue shelter using a user-centered design process to create a solution that simplifies the animal abuse reporting and investigation process.

BHCI Capstone Project, Spring 2017

Team Animal Cop: Yiran Buckley, Vanessa Kalu, Miranda Nguyen and Maggie Yu.

I was the product manager throughout this project, keeping our group rolling over the course of the semester long assignment, as well as playing a key role in building out the front-end development of the final interactive prototype.

Background

In order to help the Humane Animal Rescue shelter provide better humane investigation services and help the community, our client sought a computer-based system that supports:

  1. Residents in reporting possible abuse
  2. The shelter’s new HO in managing those cases and interacting with other agencies
  3. staff who work with animals that may been subject to abuse and who interact with potential offenders and witnesses during intake and adoptions.

We used a variety of HCI methods including interviews, contextual inquiries and literature reviews, and distilled our insights using different conceptual modeling and diagramming.

Humane Officer Dashboard

Allow Humane Officers to prioritize reports Provide location information Design for shared data

A single Humane Officer deals with a city's worth of animal abuse reports. AnimalCop supports them in prioritizing cases to do the most good.

Humane Officers are just like Police officers- constantly on a go. The Humane Officer dashboard is designed for safe mobile use and takes advantage of Google Maps API to plan their route.

Initial iterations only previewed potential routes and only displayed information about items that were in their itinerary. From feedback, we realized that there was an opportunity to help HOs manage cases by e ciently planning routes. HOs want to visit the most crucial cases and any other cases close to it for e ciency. So, in the second iteration, we added a map overview showing cases in the backlog as well as itinerary. Close enough cases could motivate the HO to visit them.

Animal rescue operations are collaborative and co-dependent.

Instances of animal abuse can coincide with other crimes. Because of this, cases requires various agencies (e.g. Animal Control, HOs, Pittsburgh Police) to work together. Breakdowns can occur in terms of communication and cooperation.

Citizen Reporting

Education is key to communication Reduce inappropriate reporting Provides Reporters feedback

The main goal of animal rescue is to educate, not confiscate.

Interviewees highlighted cases where they gave many resources to the animal owner rather than con scating the animal. It is important for HOs to develop cooperative relationships with owners and taking an educational role when possible.

Misreporting has serious consequences.

HOs and shelters have limited resources but numerous cases. Malicious, incorrect, or unspecific reports strain an already burdened officer.

Lack of feedback after reporting is frustrating and disheartening.

Citizens who report abuse are often emotionally attached and strive to understand if their actions actually impact the situation. Currently there are no follow-ups mechanisms. Reporters may attempt to get updates to no avail, become disheartened, and be discouraged from future reporting.
